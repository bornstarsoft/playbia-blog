Long-lost NES puzzle game Xcavator, originally developed in 1991 by Chris Oberth, is making a comeback thanks to a collaboration between iam8bit and the Video Game History Foundation. This fully polished cartridge edition will be available in 2025 for $100, featuring an authentic NES cart and a detailed manual that not only chronicles the games troubled past but also highlights Oberths career and the broader challenges of preserving 8-bit history.

The revival of Xcavator is a significant moment for retro gaming enthusiasts and collectors alike. It underscores the importance of preserving video game history and the stories behind the games that shaped our childhoods. With profits supporting the Video Game History Foundations archival efforts, this release is not just about nostalgia; its about ensuring that the legacy of gaming continues for future generations.

As we look forward to holding this piece of gaming history in our hands, one cant help but wonder: what other hidden gems from the past are waiting to be discovered and shared with the world?
