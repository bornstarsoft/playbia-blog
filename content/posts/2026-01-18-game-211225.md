In the ever-expanding universe of video games, free titles often get overshadowed by blockbuster releases. However, one game that has recently captured the attention of players is 12, Memory Lane, which has garnered "Very Positive" reviews on Steam since its release on December 15, 2025. This captivating indie game invites players to explore the mysterious estate of Henry Villemaine, piecing together the enigma of a vanished family through engaging puzzles and heartfelt storytellingall without spending a dime.

Developed by students at Rubika Supinfogame under the name Forget-Me-Not Studio, 12, Memory Lane is a testament to the creativity and talent emerging from academic environments. While it may be a walking simulator at heart, its intricate puzzles and emotional depth have drawn comparisons to beloved titles like What Remains of Edith Finch. The game offers a compact experience, clocking in at around an hour and 45 minutes, making it perfect for those looking for a quick yet impactful gaming session.

Despite some critiques regarding its controls and brevity, the overall reception has been overwhelmingly positive. As the gaming community continues to embrace indie titles, 12, Memory Lane serves as a reminder of the potential hidden within free games. Will we see more innovative projects from emerging developers in the future? Only time will tell.
