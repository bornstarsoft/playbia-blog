On December 19, Wordle players faced an unexpected challenge when the five-letter answer was the vowel-less word "myrrh." This unique choice left many players frustrated, as they relied on traditional strategies that prioritize vowel identification. The New York Times described the puzzle as moderately challenging, but for many, it felt like an insurmountable hurdle.

The reactions on social media were mixed, with some players expressing their disdain for the word, while others appreciated its seasonal relevance. Myrrh, a substance historically linked to Christmas, was a fitting yet tricky choice that caught many off guard. As players shared their experiences on platforms like Reddit, it became clear that this particular puzzle was more than just a game; it was a test of adaptability and creativity in the face of unexpected challenges.
