In an exciting development for the gaming industry, Arjan Brussee, co-founder of Guerilla Games and former technical director at Epic Games, is embarking on a mission to create a European alternative to popular game engines like Unreal and Unity. His new project, dubbed 'The Immense Engine', aims to offer a fully European-hosted solution that complies with local regulations and caters to a broader range of applications beyond gaming, including defense and logistics.

Brussee emphasizes the need for innovation in game engine design, particularly with the rise of artificial intelligence. He believes that existing engines are outdated, designed for traditional workflows that rely heavily on mouse interactions. With AI's capabilities, he envisions a system where intelligent agents can streamline development processes, potentially replacing the work of multiple developers. This shift could revolutionize how we create and interact with 3D worlds.

As we look to the future, the question remains: will The Immense Engine set a new standard for game development in Europe and beyond? The potential for AI-driven tools in this space could redefine not just gaming, but various industries reliant on 3D simulations.
